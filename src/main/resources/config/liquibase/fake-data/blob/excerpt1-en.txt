<p><b>Love</b> encompasses a range of strong and positive <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotion">emotional</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental_states" class="mw-redirect">mental states</a>, from the most sublime <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtue">virtue</a> or good habit, the deepest <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship">interpersonal</a> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affection">affection</a>, to the simplest <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pleasure">pleasure</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> An example of this range of meanings is that the love of a mother differs from the love of a spouse, which differs from the love of food. Most commonly, love refers to a feeling of strong attraction and emotional <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attachment_(psychology)" class="mw-redirect">attachment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oxford_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxford-3">[3]</a></sup>
